England

England is one of the four countries that make up the United Kingdom and one of the largest tourist destinations in Europe, England is a beautiful destination and offers tourists a variety of unique attractions whether it is when visiting its first-class cities such as London to enjoy its beautiful castles, so in this post you will find many of the most important and most visited attractions in England.

Big Ben Tower

The Big Ben Tower adorns the Palace of Westminster in London, as it is not just an ordinary watch, but rather an ancient cultural symbol that embodies the ancient history of the United Kingdom, and the name "Big Ben" goes beyond the limits of the clock to include the entire tower, and was named after the huge bell inside, and it is one of the most famous landmarks in the world, and its image appears frequently in movies and television programs as a symbol of Britain.Big Ben’s mighty clock tower is one of the most recognizable landmarks around the globe and one of England’s most iconic symbols. Perched on. the banks of the Thames alongside the Palace of Westminster, it’s impossible to miss while in London. The clock and tower are currently being refurbished but are set to unveil sometime in 2022.

the Tower of London

Today the Tower of London is one of England's most popular tourist attractions and was added to the World Heritage Site in 1988. The Tower of London is at the forefront of the allegedly haunted places in England and the entire British Isles, and generations have passed down a few fairy tales that the souls of those executed in the tower still roam, most notably Anne Boleyn, the second wife of King Henry VIII, Countess Margaret Pooley, and Princes Edward and Richard, the sons of King Edward IV, who are said to have disappeared in the tower. The tower was also famous for its crows that nest on its lands, which was associated with an ancient prophecy that on the day the last crows will leave the Tower of London, the throne of England will fall and with it will fall Great Britain, so a number of kings were keen to preserve the crows and their nesting grounds in the tower.Built nearly 1,000 years ago, the Tower of London is one of London’s best and most beloved historical sites. Steeped in legend and one of the oldest sites in London, it’s hard not to fall for the history and stories that surround this iconic English monument. Moreover, it’s home to the iconic Beefeaters who’ve been the ceremonial guards of the tower for centuries.

Stonehenge

Steeped in history and mystery spanning back at least 5,000 years, Stonehenge is easily one of England and Great Britain’s biggest claims to fame. As one of the most iconic prehistoric monuments in the world, it’s regularly in the top most visited monuments across England. Not only is the mystery surrounding it fascinating, but Stonehenge also takes the title for the most architecturally sophisticated stone circle in the world.Stonehenge is a prehistoric monument in Wiltshire, England, about 2 miles (3.2 km) west of Amesbury and 8 miles (13 km) north of Salisbury. Stonehenge is one of the most famous sites in the world, the remains of a ring of permanent stones within the excavation collection.
