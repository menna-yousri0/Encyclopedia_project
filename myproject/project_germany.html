Germany

Germany has a long and deep-rooted history and is among the favorite immigration destinations in the world, and receives a large number of tourists from all over the world as it includes a large part of historical cities, forests, valleys and high mountains, and visitors come to it, especially in the summer and spring months when temperatures begin to rise.One of Europe’s largest and most influential countries, from Berlin to the Black Forest, Germany is packed with iconic landmarks that every traveler should add to their list. Whether you love towering mountains or historic monuments, we’ve put together 15 of the most famous landmarks in Germany that you should visit. Step back in time at the medieval fortress of Neuschwanstein Castle, discover the symbolic significance of the Brandenburg Gate and admire at the architectural marvel of Cologne Cathedral. Or maybe traverse the picturesque landscapes of the Rhine Valley. Each offering a unique blend of history, culture and scenic beauty, there’s no shortage of iconic sites in Germany to fuel the curious mind.

The Brandenburg Gat

The Brandenburg Gate is the famous symbol of German unity. Since its completion in 1791 it has been considered the most dramatic landmark in European history. Today it is the main platform for celebrating the thirtieth anniversary of the fall of the Berlin Wall. The Brandenburg Gate in modern history is considered the scene of real events of the fall of the Berlin Wall in 1989.Symbolizing unity and peace, this iconic monument in Berlin played a significant role in Germany’s history. Built in the late 18th century and modelled on the Propylaeum of Athens’ Acropolis, it was commissioned by King Frederick William II of Prussia as a grand entrance to the city. Throughout its existence, the gate has witnessed many historic events, including Napoleonic conquests, World War II, and the division of Berlin during the Cold War. After the fall of the Berlin Wall in 1989, the Brandenburg Gate became a symbol of German reunification.

Neuschwanstein Palace

Located in Germany's picturesque Bavarian Alps, Neuschwanstein Palace is a true embodiment of fairytale charm and architectural wonders. Built by the visionary King Ludwig II, this iconic palace carries a rich history and boasts magnificent features that continue to attract visitors from all over the world.You’ll probably have seen Neuschwanstein Castle in photos – but there’s nothing like seeing it up close, as Dani confirms. “As you drive towards the castle, it looks like it’s painted onto the scenery – it’s hard to believe it’s real. The walk up to the castle, along wooded paths, is almost as magical. Then when you arrive at the castle itself, seeing those towering turrets, combined with views across the surrounding landscape, is an unforgettable experience. “We’ll also head inside the castle to see some of the interior rooms, including the Throne Room and the Lohengrin Grotto. But it’s the outside that’s truly astounding.”

Cologne Cathedral

Cologne Cathedral (German: Kölner Dom Kölner Dom (official name Hohe Domkirche St. Peter und Maria) is one of the most famous architectural and religious monuments in Germany and has a high ability to attract the largest percentage of tourists Germany has ever known. This cathedral is the true symbol of the city of Cologne, which leads many to consider the city of Cologne as a body without a soul without the cathedral «Dom». The cathedral belongs to the Catholic Church and is the seat of the Archbishop of Cologne. When its towers were completed in 1880, it was the tallest building in the world (its towers rise to 157 meters) and remained so until the construction of the Washington edifice in 1884.A masterpiece of Gothic architecture, this UNESCO World Heritage site is Germany’s most visited landmark, boasting stunning stained-glass windows and towering spires. Construction began in 1248 but wasn’t completed until 1880. Until 1884, the Cologne Cathedral was the tallest building in the world thanks to its towers which reach a height of over 500 feet. The spectacular cathedral was built to house the Shrine of the Three Kings, which is said to contain the remains of the Three Wise Men from the Gospel of St. Matthew, who followed a star to Bethlehem to present gifts to Jesus. During WWII, the cathedral was hit by 70 firebombs and 14 highly explosive bombs. Thankfully, its size and construction saved it from being utterly destroyed, as it served as a reference point for pilots flying over the city.
