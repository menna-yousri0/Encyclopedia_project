The Acropolis

Known as the "High City," it stands elevated above the Greek capital, offering a glimpse into the grandeur of ancient Greece. The Acropolis dates back to the Bronze Age, originally serving as a defensive stronghold before evolving into a significant religious and cultural center. It houses iconic structures such as the Parthenon, built in honor of the goddess Athena; the Propylaea, the monumental gateway; and the Erechtheion, renowned for its intricate design and Caryatid columns—female statues that elegantly support its porch. Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1987, the Acropolis remains a breathtaking testament to Greece’s rich history and attracts millions of visitors annually.The Acropolis of Athens is the most famous historical monument in Greece. It was constructed in the mid 5th century BC, also known as the Golden Century of Athens. Although the most famous building on the Acropolis is the Parthenon, there are many other buildings, like the Propylaea, the Erectheion and the Temple of Athena Nike. The Acropolis was entirely made of the finest Pentelic marble and by the best craftsmen of the era. Along the centuries that followed, it was largely destroyed by wars. The greatest destructions were in 1687 by a Venetian gun, and in 1816 when Lord Elgin removed many parts of the Parthenon frieze.

It seems that a team of scientists has finally discovered the temple of the god Poseidon at the site of Klyde, near the ancient village of Samicon on Greece's Peloponnese peninsula.The Temple of Poseidon looks amazing. It sits on a cliff that overlooks the Aegean Sea. This old Greek temple is built for the sea god. It reflects how strong Athens was in trade by sea and its lasting connection with the ocean. When you look at the horizon, think about the old sailors who prayed to Poseidon before their journeys. The temple was built to help sailors find their way and showed Athenian strength by protecting the important sea paths that linked Greece to other lands.

Mykonos

Mykonos is a Greek island, part of the Cyclades, located between Tinos, Syros, Paros and Naxos. The island spans an area of 85.5 km2 (33 sq mi) and rises to an altitude of 341 metres (1,119) at its highest point. There are 10,134 inhabitants (2011 census) most of whom live in a larger city, Mykonos, which is located on the west coast.Thing of a place with shining white houses that slope down to meet the blue waters of the Aegean Sea. Santorini

Santorini (Greek: Σαντορίνη), officially called Thera, is an island in the southern Aegean Sea in the Cyclades archipelago. 200 km southeast of Greece. It has a population of about 13,670 people. The Cyclades Islands are part of a mutant complex known as the Cycladic Massif.santorini is more than a well-known holiday destination—it is a geological and historical landmark shaped by one of the most powerful natural forces on Earth. Officially named Thira, this island in the southern Aegean Sea is part of a volcanic archipelago formed through repeated eruptions over millions of years. Today, it stands as the most active volcanic center in the South Aegean Volcanic Arc, with its iconic caldera offering clear evidence of past volcanic activity. The group of islands that make up Santorini—Thira, Thirassia, Nea Kameni, Palea Kameni, Aspronisi and others—are the result of 12 major eruptions, each dramatically altering the landscape. One of these, which occurred about 3,600 years ago, is among the largest eruptions in recorded history and contributed to the creation of the current caldera. Despite the destruction caused by these eruptions, the volcano has continued to reshape itself, forming new land and features over time. Today, visitors can sit at the edge of this geological wonder, with views of an active volcano just across the water.
